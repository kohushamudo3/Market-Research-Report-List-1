<p><p>The Helicobacter pylori testing market is segmented into two primary types: with endoscopy and without endoscopy. Testing with endoscopy involves direct visualization of the gastric mucosa, allowing for biopsy and precise identification of H. pylori through histological examination or rapid urease tests. Conversely, without endoscopy includes non-invasive methods, such as breath, stool, and serological tests, which offer convenience and ease of use. Both approaches cater to different clinical scenarios, balancing accuracy and patient comfort in diagnosing H. pylori infections.</p></p>
